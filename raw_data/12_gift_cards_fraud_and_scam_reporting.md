# Gift Cards, Fraud, and Scam Reporting

## Checking Gift Card Balances

Customers can check Amazon Gift Card balances through:
1. Your Account
2. Gift Cards section

Amazon stores available balances for future purchases.

## Gift Card Transaction History

Customers can review:
- Balance changes
- Transactions
- Order IDs
- Serial numbers

through the Balance Page transaction section.

## Physical Gift Card Verification

Some physical gift cards do not display printed values.

Customers can verify balances by:
- Going to Your Orders
- Matching the 16-digit serial number from the order details
- Comparing it with the serial number on the card

## Promotional Certificate Limitation

Amazon Gift Card balances do not include:
- Promotional certificates
- Promotional credits

## Gift Card Eligibility

Customers should review:
- Amazon Gift Card Terms and Conditions

to verify whether gift card balances qualify as payment methods during checkout.

## Reporting Scams and Fraud

Amazon takes fraud, phishing, spoofing, and scam attempts seriously.

Customers should report suspicious communication immediately.

## Scam Reporting Categories

Customers can report situations involving:
- Shared Amazon account information
- Shared banking information
- Remote access to devices
- Other sensitive information exposure

## Reporting Suspicious Emails

Customers without Amazon accounts can still report suspicious communications by emailing:
- reportascam@amazon.com

Amazon recommends:
- Sending suspicious messages as attachments for investigation purposes

## Reporting Calls and Text Messages

Customers can report suspicious:
- Phone calls
- SMS messages
- Text messages

to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) through:
- https://reportfraud.ftc.gov

## Reporting Suspicious Activity

Customers can also report:
- Suspicious products
- Suspicious sellers
- Brushing scams
- Unsolicited packages
- Gift card fraud

# About Items Shipped to Companies Not Affiliated with Amazon



Shipping items to a third-party company that isn’t affiliated with Amazon may result in complications not covered by Amazon.



Third-party companies that are not affiliated with Amazon include freight forwards, Buyer’s Clubs, third-party warehouses, and re-sellers. Amazon isn't responsible for any loss that occurs to items after the items are delivered to a third-party business and cannot issue refunds or replace these items.



For more information about freight forwarding, go to About Freight Forwarders and Hand Carry. For all other third-party companies:



* If an item arrives from Amazon with damaged packaging, please instruct the third-party company to refuse delivery. We'll issue a refund when the item is returned to Amazon.
* If an item arrives defective or damaged inside the packaging, go to Your Orders to view return options.
* If you received your package, but the item was missing from the box, go to Find a Missing Item from Your Order.
* If you have a missing package that shows as delivered, check with your third-party company. Follow the steps in Find a Missing Package that Shows as Delivered.



Amazon will not engage with a third-party company that did not make a purchase directly from Amazon.



Contact Amazon Customer Service if:



* The carrier delivered the item to the wrong address.
* You ordered a non-returnable item that was shipped in multiple boxes, and you only received a partial shipment.
